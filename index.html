The apps are not the main goal. You are using the apps to practise habits that matter in real life: planning, communication, creative judgement, digital organisation, problem-solving, and self-management.

Knowledge

Leadership

Communication

Organisation

You learn digital structure

You will use folders, file names, trackers, and links so your work does not get lost.

Creativity

You will create videos, designs, mood boards, guides, comics, edits, or posts with a clear purpose.

Real-world confidence

How This Helps in the Real World

These projects are fun, but they are also training you for real life. You are practising how people work in companies, creative studios, schools, businesses, and online platforms.

Project management

You learn how to manage work

You will break a big idea into smaller tasks, set dates, track status, and finish work instead of only talking about ideas.

Digital organisation

You learn how to keep files properly

Drive folders, file names, links, and trackers teach you how to keep your work organised and easy to find.

Communication

You learn how to explain your thinking

Captions, updates, scripts, Discord guides, family check-ins, and reflections help you communicate clearly.

Creative judgement

You learn how to choose a direction

Mood boards and examples help you decide what looks good, what feels clear, and what fits your project.

Confidence

Independence

House Rules

Four rules that keep the project healthy and fun. The first one is the big one.

Rule 01 — the big one

Your only competition is last-week you.

Four different projects, four different lanes. We will never compare your numbers to your brothers' — or to Baba's. Each of you is measured only against your own first week and your own craft.

Rule 02

Shipped beats perfect

A finished piece you shared beats a perfect idea stuck in your head.

Rule 03

Your project, your call

You own it. Mum & Baba are sponsors, not editors. They ask; you make.

Rule 04

Stuck is the job

Getting stuck isn't failing — it's the work. Mark it Stuck, ask for help, and keep going. Never quit silently.

Parent Review System

For Mum & Baba. Keep the check-ins short, consistent, and calm. The aim is ownership, not micromanagement.

Weekly Check-In

Ask four questions

  • What did you complete this week?
  • Where is it saved?
  • What did you learn?
  • What is the next task?
Progress Evidence

Look for proof

  • Updated tracker or checklist.
  • Saved files in the correct folder.
  • Drafts, edits, posts, guides, or screenshots.
  • One improvement from last week.
Boundaries

Protect safety and balance

  • Review public account settings.
  • Approve public-facing posts when needed.
  • No pressure to show face or voice.
  • Keep sessions short and sustainable.

Core principle

The success measure is not viral content, perfect design, or a polished presentation. Success means each boy practised planning, creating, publishing or sharing, improving, and taking more ownership than before.

10

Publishing and Safety Rules

Publishing is part of the project, but it must be done safely and with family approval.

Before anything goes public, check these rules

  • Do not show your full name, school, home, location, private family details, or personal information.
  • Do not film strangers’ faces or private license plates. If they appear, blur or remove them.
  • Ask permission before filming in a showroom or any private place.
  • Use family-approved accounts, usernames, captions, and sharing spaces.
  • Do not reply to strange messages, arguments, or requests. Show Mum & Baba.
  • For Islamic AI stories, do not invent religious facts, do not depict prophets or sacred figures directly, and review sensitive content before sharing.
  • For AI tools, treat generated content as a draft. You still need to check accuracy, tone, and safety.
  • Publishing is not about becoming viral. It is about learning consistency, communication, and improvement.
