House rules

Four rules for the studio.

Rule 01 — the big one

Your only competition is last-week you.

Three different channels, three different lanes. We will never compare your numbers to your brothers'. Each of you is measured against your own first week and your own craft.

Rule 02

Ship beats perfect

A posted video beats a perfect idea stuck in your head.

Rule 03

Your channel, your call

You own it. We're stakeholders, not editors. We ask; you make.

Rule 04

Stuck is the job

Getting stuck isn't failing — it's the work. Ask, don't quit.

For Mum & Baba — keep this tab for yourselves

How to run it without running it.

A few quiet reminders so the project does its developmental work — not just its surface work.

Stay stakeholdersAsk questions, don't take over. The instant you reorganise a boy's sheet or edit his video for him, it stops being his — and the ownership is the whole point. Messy-but-his wins.
Guard Rule 01Protect the no-comparison rule hardest of all. Mohammad's numbers will dwarf his brothers' early. Never praise one boy by contrast to another — measure each only against his own week one.
Set the dateLock Premiere Night out loud, early. It's the deadline the whole thing hangs on. A real date turns this from an idea into a project.
Let them get stuckFriction is the curriculum. When a boy is blocked, ask "what's the blocker?" — don't solve it for him. The struggle is where the growth lives.
The rewardChoose something that points back at the craft. A tool upgrade, an outing, or kit beats cash — cash quietly turns this into a numbers race, which fights Rule 01.
